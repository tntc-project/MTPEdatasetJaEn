Guidance on "Support System for Single-Parent Families"
For details, please ask at the inquiries contact below.
Inquiries will be handled in Japanese.
Receiving allowances or pension
Child Rearing Allowance/Nagoya City Single Parent Family Allowance/Aichi Prefecture Orphan Allowance
This allowance is paid if you are raising a child under the age of 18 by yourself due to divorce, children born out of wedlock, or death, etc.
*Includes cases where the father or mother has a certain disability.
*If the child is 18 years old, you are eligible for the allowance from the child's birthday to March 31st.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Child allowance
This allowance is paid to those who are raising children up to the third year of junior high school.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Bereaved family basic pension
This allowance is paid in cases such as when a person who has been enrolled in the National Pension or Old-age Basic Pension for 25 years or more has died, and when their widow/widower lives with a child aged 18 or under.
*If the child is 18 years old, you are eligible for the allowance from the child's birthday to March 31st.
Inquiries: Management Office, Insurance and Pension Section, Ward Office, Insurance Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Reduce financial burden
Subsidy for medical expenses for single-parent families
Self-payment of medical expenses is subsidized.
Inquiries: Insurance Office, Insurance and Pension Section, Ward Office, Insurance Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Reduction/exemption of national health insurance premiums
Widows and single parents under the tax law pay reduced national health insurance premiums or are exempt.
*Conditions apply.
Inquiries: Insurance Office, Insurance and Pension Section, Ward Office, Insurance Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
National health insurance fee exemption/deferral
Widows and single parents under the tax law are exempted from or can defer national health insurance premiums.
Conditions apply.
Inquiries: Management Office, Insurance and Pension Section, Ward Office, Insurance Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Reduction/exemption of water and sewerage charges
Water and sewerage charges are reduced for households receiving Child Rearing Allowance.
*Limited to cases where the recipient himself/herself holds the contract with water and sewerage company.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
JR commuter pass special discount
Households receiving Child Rearing Allowance can receive a 30% discount by submitting a certificate issued by a ward office or branch office when they purchase a JR commuter pass.
*Student commuter passes are not eligible.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Receive tax reduction/exemption
Those who meet the conditions under the tax law can benefit from the tax reduction system.
In addition, those who meet the conditions may be exempted from or pay a reduced rate of inhabitant tax (city/prefectural tax).
If you want to apply, please do not forget to declare it in your year-end tax adjustment and final tax return.
Inquiries: City tax office of your ward
Receive a loan

You can borrow necessary expenses for studying at school, acquiring skills, finding a job, or moving house, etc.

Receive child care support
Short-term admission life support for children (short stay)
If it is temporarily difficult to raise a child at home due to social reasons such as parental illness, in principle, the child will be looked after at an infant home, children's home, or foster home for up to one week.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Life support for single-parent families, etc.
For single-parent households and widows who are having trouble with daily life
Living assistance: A helper visits your home to help with housework and nursing care.
Child-rearing support: Temporarily take care of your child at a designated child-care facility.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Diverse childcare
If you need childcare for your child due to work or other reasons, you can use a nursery school.
Holiday daycare, extended daycare, nighttime daycare, sick child daycare, and post-sick child daycare are available.
Inquiries: Public Welfare and Child Affairs Office, Public Welfare and Child Affairs Section, Ward Office, Protection and Child Affairs Office, Resident Welfare Section, Branch Office
Extended childcare in kindergarten

Inquiries: Your local kindergarten or certified children center
Temporary childcare
We temporarily take care of children if childcare at home becomes temporarily difficult due to unforeseen circumstances, or in order to reduce the mental and physical burden on guardians due to childcare fatigue.
